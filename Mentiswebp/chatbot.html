Personality Disorders

Personality disorders are mental health conditions that affect the way a person thinks, feels, and behaves. They can cause significant impairment in relationships and daily functioning. Other common personality disorders include:

  • Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD): Characterized by a disregard for others' rights and a lack of empathy.
  • Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD): Characterized by a grandiose sense of self-importance, a need for admiration, and a lack of empathy for others.
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD): Characterized by a preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, and control.
  • Schizoid Personality Disorder: Characterized by a lack of interest in social relationships, a tendency towards solitary activities, and emotional coldness.
